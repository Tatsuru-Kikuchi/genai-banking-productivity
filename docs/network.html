Critical Finding

Algorithmic Coupling in the Systemic Core

θ = 3.13 (large) vs θ = 0.15 n.s. (small)

Large banks exhibit 20× stronger spillovers than small banks, indicating that AI adoption creates synchronized profitability across the systemic core. This "algorithmic coupling" amplifies positive shocks in normal times but represents a potential contagion channel under stress.

Economic Mechanisms

Why Spillovers Differ by Size

Large Banks — Strong Coupling

  • Shared AI vendor ecosystems (Azure, AWS)
  • Correlated investment in similar technologies
  • Interconnected through capital markets
  • Executive labor market spillovers

Small Banks — Isolated

  • Limited AI investment capacity
  • Rely on third-party fintech providers
  • Geographically isolated operations
  • Less interconnected in capital markets

Systemic Risk Implications

  • AI creates new contagion channel among large banks
  • Model risk correlated across institutions
  • Vendor concentration creates single points of failure
